2 Cookie Policy

Occasionally, we will place a small data file on your computer or mobile device to ensure the normal operation of our website, and such file is called a cookie. Cookie is a simple text file stored by the web server on your computers or mobile devices. Only the server can retrieve or read the contents of the Cookie. For a browser, each Cookie is unique. It includes certain anonymous information such as unique identifiers, website names, and some digits or numbers. It allows websites to remember information such as your preferences or shopping cart contents.

Most of the large websites and Internet service providers will use cookies during your visits ("conversation Cookie") or revisits ("persistent Cookie") so that the websites can "remember" your preferences in order to improve the user experiences. Cookies can help websites to remember your settings as well as language, font size, and other preferences when you browse a website using your computer or mobile devices so that you would not need to re-enter these settings each time you visit. If a certain website doesn't use cookies, you will be treated as a new visitor when you move to a new page in this site. For example, after you have entered detailed data and moved on to another page, the webpage would not be able to identify you and cannot let you stay in a logged-in state.

Our cookies won't be used to capture your personal identification but will only be used to enhance your webpage operations and deliver greater convenience.
